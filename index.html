A community-led initiative for camera transparency labels

What is CamInfo?

CamInfo is a community-led initiative that helps individuals and organizations communicate the presence and capabilities of video cameras through standardized "Camera Nutrition Labels." We're making it easy for camera owners to be transparent about what their cameras do, and for people to understand what data is being collected in spaces they visit.

Our Inspiration: CamInfo is inspired by the IoT Security & Privacy Label initiative led by researchers at Carnegie Mellon University. While there's no formal affiliation between us, we share the belief that transparent labeling helps build trust. Learn more about their work.

We're also driven by:

  • The need for greater transparency about what modern camera systems can actually do
  • A commitment to helping people understand how their faces and images are being captured, processed, and potentially stored
  • The desire to promote respectful technology practices that balance security needs with privacy rights

Why use camera labels?

In an increasingly surveilled world, transparency builds trust. Camera labels:

  • Help people make informed choices about the spaces they enter
  • Allow camera owners to demonstrate responsible data practices
  • Create a standard way to communicate complex technical capabilities
  • Promote ethical use of surveillance technology

How it works

Each CamInfo label includes critical information about:

  • Video and audio capabilities
  • Motion detection and facial recognition usage
  • Data storage location and security
  • Retention periods
  • Access controls and contact information

Labels can be displayed physically near cameras or linked via QR codes to provide detailed information.
